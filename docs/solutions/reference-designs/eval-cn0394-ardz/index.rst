EVAL-CN0394-ARDZ
=================

Multi-Channel Thermocouple Temperature Meter.

.. toctree::
   :hidden:

   hardware
   software

Overview
--------

:adi:`CN0394` is a single-supply, multi-channel thermocouple temperature meter
with RTD cold junction compensation. The system supports B, E, J, K, N, R, S,
and T type thermocouples across 4 independent channels with full range
temperature measurement.

The circuit uses the :adi:`ADuCM360` low-power precision analog microcontroller
with dual sigma-delta ADCs and ARM Cortex-M3 core. The internal ADC has
2 channels available, 24-bit resolution with PGA and reference. The first
channel is used for RTD measurements, while the second channel measures
thermocouple changes.

   :width: 650px
   :align: center

   EVAL-CN0394-ARDZ board (front view).

.. figure:: cn0394_demo_1.png
   :width: 650px
   :align: center

   EVAL-CN0394-ARDZ connected to EVAL-ADICUP360.

The EVAL-CN0394-ARDZ board has 4 thermocouple ports (**P1** to **P4**) with
4 Pt1000 RTDs (**R1** to **R4**) close to each thermocouple socket for cold
junction compensation. The RTD operates in 2-wire mode and the excitation
current is generated by the ADuCM360.

The application reads the 4 RTD channels and the 4 thermocouple channels,
processes them, and makes all necessary calculations to output a linearized
temperature for each available port. Linearization uses standard formulas
generated by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).

Required Equipment
------------------

- EVAL-CN0394-ARDZ evaluation board (Arduino shield)
- :adi:`EVAL-ADICUP360` development board
- Up to 4 thermocouples (any combination of B, E, J, K, N, R, S, T types)
- Micro USB to USB cable
- PC with Microsoft Windows 7 64-bit or later and USB 2.0 port or higher
- Serial terminal software (such as PuTTY or Tera Term)

For detailed hardware setup, see :doc:`hardware`.
For software demo and algorithms, see :doc:`software`.
