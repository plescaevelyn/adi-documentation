Hardware
========

DAQ Board
---------

High speed data acquisition board containing the :adi:`AD9094` quad channel
ADC and clocking control for the full system. The FMC compliant connector
interface means that this board can be connected to the user's preferred FPGA
development board. The Laser board and AFE board connect directly to this
board.

.. figure:: daq_board.png
   :align: center
   :width: 500

   DAQ Board block diagram

The :adi:`AD9094` is a quad, 8-bit, 1 GSPS ADC. It supports JESD204B lane
rates up to 15 Gbps, four integrated wideband decimation filters, numerically
controlled oscillator blocks, and is programmable via an SPI interface.

The IC selection for clock signal generation ensures low phase-noise,
programmable delays for proper deterministic latency, and low power
consumption. The :adi:`ADF4360-7` is a PLL which takes a crystal-generated
25 MHz input and outputs a low phase-noise 1 GHz clock signal. This signal is
then fed to an :adi:`AD9528`, which outputs five clock signals as required by
the ADC and the JESD204B interface. The AD9528 can divide frequencies and/or
delay clock signals, thus ensuring synchronization for deterministic latency.
The AD9528 is used in buffer mode to reduce power consumption; in this mode the
internal PLLs are disabled and the AD9528 acts as a clock distribution chip.

.. figure:: daq_clocking.jpg
   :align: center
   :width: 500

   DAQ Board clocking scheme

The stringent noise challenges posed by the 1 GSPS analog-to-digital
conversion are met by ultralow noise regulators such as the :adi:`ADP7156` and
:adi:`ADP7159`. All the power for this board is derived from the 3.3 V and
12 V supplies coming off the FPGA board via the FMC connector.

The ADC input creates a 100 Ohm differential impedance. The external components
account for the equivalent circuit of the analog inputs of the ADC. The user
has the option to add capacitors to filter out both differential and common mode
noise voltages.

.. figure:: adc_input.jpg
   :align: center
   :width: 500

   ADC input equivalent circuit

The DAQ board meets the VITA 57 design specs for width but not for length.

Laser Board
-----------

The laser board is mated to the AFE board for mechanical mounting on an
optional tripod stand. It is then connected electrically to the DAQ board
using the cable provided. It contains four
`Osram SPL LL90_3 <https://www.osram.com/ecat/Radial%20Smart%20Laser%20SPL%20LL90_3/com/en/class_pim_web_catalog_103489/global/prd_pim_device_2220017/>`__
Radial Smart Lasers.

.. figure:: laser_board.png
   :align: center
   :width: 500

   Laser Board block diagram

All lasers are driven simultaneously for an increase in beam strength giving
longer range. A PWM signal generated by the FPGA carrier board with
programmable pulse width and frequency is used to control the lasers. The signal
is generated on the FPGA as LVDS to make it less susceptible to noise as it
travels from the FPGA to the Laser board through the DAQ board and the ribbon
cable. It is converted on the Laser board to single-ended mode using the
:adi:`ADN4691E`, an M-LVDS transceiver. The single-ended signal is then fed to
the :adi:`ADP3634`, a high current, dual high-speed driver. Each output can
source up to 4 A and the typical rise and fall times are 10 ns at a 2.2 nF
load.

The drive signal can be fed back to the ADC for time-of-flight reference. An
:adi:`ADA4930-1`, a very low noise, low distortion, high speed differential
amplifier, is used for the single-ended to differential conversion.

An external power supply is used to power the lasers. The kit comes with a
12 V at 4 A supply. For safety reasons the power to the lasers is turned on
only by pressing the S1 switch.

AFE Board
---------

The AFE board receives the optical reflected signal, converts it to an
electrical signal, and transfers it to the ADC on the DAQ board.

.. figure:: afe_board.png
   :align: center
   :width: 500

   AFE Board block diagram

The optical signal is converted using a 16 channel
`APD array from First Sensor <https://www.first-sensor.com/en/products/optical-sensors/detectors/avalanche-photodiode-arrays-apd-arrays/>`__.
The 16 current outputs are fed to four :adi:`LTC6561` low-noise, four-channel
transimpedance amplifiers (TIA) with 220 MHz bandwidth. They feature 74 kOhm
transimpedance gain and 30 uA linear input current range. The measurement range
can be increased from 30 uA to at least 3 mA. An internal 4-to-1 MUX is used
to select the output channel. The output voltage is single-ended and swings
2 Vpp.

.. figure:: lidar_tia_signal_chain.jpg
   :align: center
   :width: 500

   AFE Board signal chain

The single-ended output is converted to a differential voltage using an
:adi:`ADA4950-1`. The differential gain is set to 1 and the appropriate common
mode voltage (Vcm) is applied. A negative feedback circuit featuring the
:adi:`LT6016` rail-to-rail input operational amplifiers and the :adi:`ADR3525`
low power, high precision CMOS voltage reference reduces the no-signal
differential voltage. The :adi:`LT8331` is used to generate the bias voltage
for the APD, which is programmable between -120 V and -300 V. Additionally, the
APD temperature is measured, enabling the adjustment of the bias voltage for
maximum APD SNR regardless of temperature.
