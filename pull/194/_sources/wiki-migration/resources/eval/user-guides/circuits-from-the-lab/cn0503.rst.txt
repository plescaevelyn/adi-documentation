EVAL-CN0503-ARDZ Hardware User Guide
====================================

.. important::

   We are in the process of migrating our documentation to GitHub Pages.

   
   The latest version of this page can be found at https://analogdevicesinc.github.io/documentation/solutions/reference-designs/eval-cn0503-ardz/index.html

| :adi:`CN0503` is a reconfigurable multi-parameter optical liquid measurement platform capable of performing colorimetry, turbidity, and fluorometry. The design minimizes complexity by using a highly integrated multimodal sensor front end capable of simultaneously driving four LEDs, and synchronously measuring four pairs of photodiodes at a flexible sampling rate. Furthermore, the front end has on-chip digital filters and high ambient light rejection which allow the platform to operate regardless of environmental lighting conditions. |image1|

LED and Photodiode Boards
-------------------------

| The :adi:`CN0503` supports 4 channels or paths of light which can be configured for different types of optical measurements. The 2 channels at the sides are capable of measuring light intensity reflected at 90 degrees from the liquid sample to support measurements for turbidity, fluorescence, etc. The LED boards placed in each of the 4 channels are interchangeable and natively support 8 different wavelengths. Additional spare boards are available to accommodate LEDs with other wavelengths. |image44|

Assembling the Mechanical Fixtures
----------------------------------

| :adi:`CN0503` uses 3D printed mechanical fixtures to enable four directed light paths to the liquid sample held in a `Cuvette <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_. The mechanical fixtures can be classified into two separate objects: the \*\* `Mounted Base <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_\ \*\* and the **Tower**. The `Mounted Base <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ is capable of holding four **Towers** which are freely interchangeable, as well as two `Fluorescent Filters <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_. The **Tower** holds the **Cuvette**, which contains the liquid sample, and optical glass items: a `Condenser Lens <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_, a `Neutral Density Filter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_, a `Narrowband Transmit Filter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_, and a `Beamsplitter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_. Below are two videos showing the assembly of the base and tower, respectively.

Connecting the LED and Photodiode (PD) Boards
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

The `LED Boards <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ are drawn with a huge 'X' on the silkscreen which marks the center of the area of interest or the active area of the LED. To mount the `LED Boards <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_, face the top of the board with the LED facing towards the small window of the `Mounted Base <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ and attach the 4-pin female connectors to the jumper headers marked LED as shown below.

|image58|

.. important::

   Be sure to check if the 'X' mark is centered at the middle of the small
   window of the base

| To mount the photodiode (PD) boards, face the top of the board with the photodiode facing towards the small window of the base and attach the 4-pin female connectors to the jumper headers marked PD as shown below. |image59| |image60|

.. important::

   The two `Fluorescent PD Boards <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ should be placed at opposite sides of the base. The exact centering of the photodiode is not crucial

.. tip::

   To replace or remove the PD and LED boards, pull the the notch in each board
   using a flat screw driver or fingernail to lift it up from the jumper headers

Assembling the Tower
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

| The tower holds the optical glass items and the cuvette which holds the liquid sample. This section details how each of these items are assembled into position to form the light path and also, the 3D printed mechanical fixtures which hold them in place (`Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_, `Cuvette Holder Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_, `Beamsplitter Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_, and `Lens Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_).

.. tip::

   Following the proper order of steps to assemble the tower makes it easier to
   do as well as prioritizes the important optical glass pieces.

Securing the Beamsplitter
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

The `Beamsplitter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ is used to direct the light path to both the liquid sample in the `Cuvette <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ as well as the reference photodiode located below it. It is angled at 45 degrees with respect to the light path and should be held tightly in place. Slight movements of the `Beamsplitter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ will affect the measurement ratios obtained from the transmit photodiode and reference photodiode.

-  Starting with the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_, insert two `Hex Nuts <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ to each of the two circular depressions as shown below. These should be completely pressed and held tight to the depression to avoid the hassle of them coming off later.

|image62|

   \* Insert the `Beamsplitter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ in the angled 45 degree slit. Be careful in applying pressure as this may damage the part.

   |image63|

   \* Secure the `Beamsplitter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ with the `Beamsplitter Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ as shown below.

   |image64|

   \* Screw the `Beamsplitter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ to the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ using 2 `S1 Screws <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ at the two holes as shown below. Be sure to drive the screws in until the head no longer protrudes from the depression.

   |image65|

.. tip::

   Check for slight movements of `Beamsplitter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ by listening for clicking sounds when you shake the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_.

Securing the Condenser Lens
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

The `Condenser Lens <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ is used to focus and direct the light from the LED to through the tower and form the light path. Thus, it is important that the `Condenser Lens <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ be positioned properly and tightly secured so that no slight movements occur.

-  Attach 2 `Hex Nuts <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ to each of the two circular depressions on the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ as shown below. These should be completely pressed and held tight to the depression to avoid the hassle of them coming off later.

|image66|

   \* Place the `Condenser Lens <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ on the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_. This will be easier to do by rotating the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ vertically so that the `Condenser Lens <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ will drop in position.

   |image67|

   \* Press the `Lens Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ to the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ and screw it in place using an `S2 Screw <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ at the bottom hole (marked green) and an `S1 Screw <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ at the top hole (marked red) as show below.

   |image68|

.. tip::

   Check for slight movements of the lens by listening for clicking sounds when you shake the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_.

Adding a Narrowband Transmit Filter and Neutral Density Filter (Optional)
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

.. note::

   This step is typically not required, because the EVAL-CN0503-ARDZ kit does not contain these components. Both the `Narrowband Transmit Filter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ and `Neutral Density Filter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ are optional items which can be added to the optical path if your application requires it.

-  The `Narrowband Transmit Filter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ is used to select specific wavelengths of light from a wideband LED. Place the filter in to the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ as shown below. The placement does not need to be as secure and tight as the `Beamsplitter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ and `Condenser Lens <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_.

|image69|

   \* The `Neutral Density Filter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ is used to decrease the light intensity for a wide range of wavelengths. For instances wherein the LED light intensity easily saturates the photodiode current input to the :adi:`ADPD4101`, adding the `Neutral Density Filter <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ will help decrease the input current range.

   |image70|

Placing the Cuvette Cover
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

The `Cuvette Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ secures the hold for the `Cuvette <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ and shields the measurement light path from external sources. It also prevents the optional filters to be knocked out of the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_. Thus, it is necessary to lock the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ as detailed below.

-  Insert a `Hex Nut <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ in the depression in the `Cuvette Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_. This should be completely pressed and held tight to the depression to avoid the hassle of it coming off later.

|image71|

   \* Place the `Cuvette Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ on top of the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ as shown below

   |image72|

   \* Secure the `Cuvette Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ using an `S3 Screw <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ at the specified hole shown below.

   |image73|

Placing the Cuvette
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

| The `Cuvette <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ can easily be added to the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ as shown below.
| |image74|
| **(Optional)** The `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ is a tight fit for 10mm by 10mm `Cuvette <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_. When using a smaller-sized cuvette, a provisional screw hole can be used to press against the `Cuvette <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ and secure it.

-  Remove the `Cuvette Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ and insert a `Hex Nut <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ in the depression in the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ as shown below. This should be completely pressed and held tight to the depression to avoid the hassle of it coming off later.

|image75|

   \* Replace the `Cuvette Cover <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ and attach an `S2 Screw <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ at the specified hole in the `Cuvette Holder <https://wiki.analog.com/>`_ as shown below. The depth of the screw can be adjusted to increase or decrease the tightness of the hold.

Optical Path 1
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

In optical path 1, the experiment shows the detection of quinine in tonic water
using fluorescence measurements. Optionally, you can also use samples of soda
water and de-ionized water for comparison.

-  :doc:`Optical Platform: Fluorescence Measurement Demo (ADICUP3029 + EVAL-CN0503-ADRZ) </wiki-migration/resources/eval/user-guides/circuits-from-the-lab/cn0503/fluorescence>`

Optical Path 2
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

In optical path 2, the experiment shows pH measurement by using colorimetry to
detect changes the solution through the bromothymol blue indicator. As pH
changes from an acidic level to a neutral and basic level, bromothymol blue
changes color from yellow to blue. A yellow colored solution will block 430nm
light while a blue colored solution will allow it to pass. The change in pH can
be done using a simple aquarium kit.

-  :doc:`Optical Platform: pH Measurement Demo (ADICUP3029 + EVAL-CN0503-ADRZ) </wiki-migration/resources/eval/user-guides/circuits-from-the-lab/cn0503/colorimetry>`

Optical Path 3
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

In optical path 3, the experiment shows nitrate detection using colorimetry.
Using the API reagent found in nitrate test kits, a solution with a higher
concentration of nitrate turns into a darker shade of red. As a result, the
degree of absorbance of 615nm light decreases with the increasing nitrate
concentration.

-  :doc:`Optical Platform: Nitrate Measurement Demo (ADICUP3029 + EVAL-CN0503-ADRZ) </wiki-migration/resources/eval/user-guides/circuits-from-the-lab/cn0503/nitrate-measurement>`
   ==== Optical Path 4 ====

In optical path 4, the experiment shows turbidity measurements of cloudy water
made by adding a a drop of milk and de-ionized water using 530nm light.

-  :doc:`Optical Platform: Turbidity Measurement Demo (ADICUP3029 + EVAL-CN0503-ADRZ) </wiki-migration/resources/eval/user-guides/circuits-from-the-lab/cn0503/turbidity>`
